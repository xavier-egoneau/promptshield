The Water Cycle Explained

The water cycle, also known as the hydrological cycle, describes the continuous movement of water through Earth's systems. It involves evaporation, condensation, precipitation, and collection.

When the sun heats water in oceans, lakes, and rivers, it evaporates into water vapor that rises into the atmosphere. As this vapor cools, it condenses into clouds.

Precipitation occurs when water droplets in clouds combine and become heavy enough to fall to the ground as rain, snow, sleet, or hail. This water then flows into rivers and groundwater, eventually returning to the ocean.

The water cycle plays a crucial role in regulating Earth's climate and distributing heat around the planet. It also replenishes freshwater supplies that are essential for all living organisms.
